Master of Science in Energy Engineering

Designed for engineering graduates and early-career professionals looking to master power systems management, grid resilience, and emerging alternative energy technologies.

12 Core Credits Complete 4 comprehensive foundational engineering courses.
18 Elective Credits Customize your trajectory with optional specialized program emphases.
Applied Project Track Synthesize learning with a 3-member faculty committee and an industrial capstone project.

Fundamentals of Energy Engineering Graduate Certificate

A perfect fit for working professionals who desire immediate energy sector credentials without committing to a full degree. This option serves as a stackable pathway that can later be fully applied toward the MS.

Requirement Check: Complete the 4 Core Courses (12 credits) + 3 approved technical elective credits.

Prerequisite Pathway for Transitioning STEM Majors

Applicants without an engineering degree but possessing robust quantitative backgrounds (e.g., Mathematics, Physics, Economics, Applied Computing) are welcome to enter through completing essential foundational milestones:

  • Thermodynamics
  • Fluid Mechanics
  • Electric Circuits
  • Computer Programming
