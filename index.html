Master of Science in Energy Engineering

Designed for engineering graduates and early-career professionals looking to master power systems management, grid resilience, and emerging alternative energy technologies.

12 Core Credits Complete 4 comprehensive foundational engineering courses at the 6000-level.
18 Elective Credits Customize your trajectory with optional specialized program emphases.
Applied Project Track Synthesize learning with a 3-member faculty committee and an industrial capstone project (ENRG 6900/6910).

Fundamentals of Energy Engineering Graduate Certificate

A perfect fit for working professionals who desire immediate energy sector credentials without committing to a full degree. This option serves as a stackable pathway that can later be fully applied toward the MS.

Requirement Check: Complete the 4 Core Courses at the 6000-level (12 credits) + 3 approved technical elective credits (15 credits total).

Undergraduate Minor in Energy Engineering

Designed for current undergraduate engineering students who want to expand their studies with technical knowledge and specialized applications tailored directly to the energy sector.

Requirement Check: Complete the 4 Core Courses at the 5000-level (12 credits) + 4 approved technical elective credits (16 credits total).
